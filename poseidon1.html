Ripple

Our platform targets 2 weaknesses with current social media:

  1. Exposure

    It's difficult to reach new audiences.
    Current social media platforms restrict people’s reaches to clusters of friends and followers, or people close by. Ripple enables users to break out of these restrictive networks by spreading their content to new people nearby and friends around the world. Both segments of this network can spread content to even more people near them; this method of sharing helps ripples travel across our unbounded global network. Furthermore, our map feature allows users to visualize how their posts spread.


    Drew rippled a picture of himself doing a backflip in Utah, and people near him spread it. His follower in Germany decided to spread it to people around her too. Drew’s picture began to travel throughout the US and Europe simultaneously. Eventually, it rippled around the entire world.

  2. Content Relevance

    Social media networks force users to sift through irrelevant content to find "the good stuff."
    On Ripple, content only travels so far and so long as it is relevant. If a user spreads a post, its score goes up. We allow users to spread all posts, but we display the content with the higher scores first. Since posts reach people outside a user’s personal network, Ripplers are constantly sharing and discovering ripples from across the globe. Ripplers also have slightly different feeds based on friends, location, and time, so each user experiences the worlds we live in differently.
