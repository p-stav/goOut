Ripple - who we are

We see 2 problems with current social media:

  1. Exposure - It's hard for people to reach new audiences

    There are people and businesses who want to promote their thoughts, work, and events. Their voices are bounded in clusters of their friends, followers, or people in close proximity.

                   

  2. Impact - People want to see their voice travel.

    There is no good way to see real-time, geographical feedback on content and to visualize how users influence each other on these networks

Our solution is Ripple
A network using a function of time, proximity, and engagement to spread content

How it works:


  1. Start a "ripple", or post. It first goes to people nearby.

  2. Those who receive a ripple can spread it to others near them, or dismiss it.

  3. Ripples reach more people as users continue to spread them.
    They travel based on a function of time, proximity, and the number of spreads.
Ripple maps how content spreads and allows users to comment.

Ripple changes the way information spreads.
Information travels freely; content previously restricted within clusters now diffuses through our system so long as it is relevant.


Our Vision

Reach new audiences
Ripple's spreading mechanism makes it easier for quality content to reach new audiences.

  • One can download Ripple and immediately broadcast a message to a larger number of users. Consumers and brands no longer need to amass a friend/follower system to make impressions.

  • Our vision includes allowing users to follow others whose content they enjoy, and to filter content they view to match their interests. This will allow people to interact with audience members and topics they are interested in.
Allow users to understand their impact
Likes, retweets, upvotes, etc are 1-dimensional measures of engagement. Each Ripple visualizes how many times it was spread, where it has traveled to, and how it got there.

  • Consumers love interacting with these ripple maps to see how content reached them and how far their messages travel. It's a key differentiator between us and other competitors.

  • Businesses can determine the effectiveness of their brand, analyze the success for different geographical markets across the world, and learn more about the users which spread their information.
Surface engaging content
Ripple will surface engaging content and information on both a local and global level. Posts relevant to a city will reach users in that area, while globally appealing content will travel further.

  • Local exploration
    We are well positioned to present users deals and promotions in their vicinity, local news and emergencies, and upcoming events. This information will travel so far and long as it's relevant, whether it has neighborhood-wide, city-wide, or global appeal.

  • Global Content
    Users can view the most engaging content from around the world. They will also be able to pan over a map to any location on Earth and find real-time analysis of trending ripples and current events in that area.

Ripple has 3 types of users

Artists + photographers    

These users are artists who ripple their own work.

They are photographers, bloggers, and painters/sculptors who are looking for exposure and validation.

Users who share their viewpoints    

These users ripple thoughts and viewpoints on current events. They want to spark a conversation.

Users who share spontaneous moments    

These users ripple images showcasing their in-the-moment experiences. These include scenic views, pets, and other things they come across.
